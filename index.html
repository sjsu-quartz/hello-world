The Institute of Queer Ecology (IQECO) is a collaborative organism looking to find and create alternatives. The solutions to environmental degradation are found on the periphery and we seek to bring them to the forefront of public consciousness. Guided by queer and feminist theory and decolonial thinking, we work to undo dangerously destructive human-centric hierarchiesâ€”or even flip themâ€”to look at the critical importance of things happening invisibly; underground and out of sight.

Common Survival Exhibition

Febrary-April, PRIDE Center, Diaz Compean Student Union

IQECO's Common Survival is a multiformat publication, contained within an 11 x 17 x 5 inch box, that looks to answer the question: Given our current state of disaster, how do we survive moving forward? The 33 projects included appear as texts, zines, photographs, sculptural objects, video/audio recordings and other hybridized modes of research display. The box will be installed in the PRIDE Center alongside an empty container for contributions from the SJSU community.

About

QuArtz, the Queer Arts Event Series, aims to build visibility for LGBTQ+ issues and community through arts programming while cultivating connections and support amongst LGBTQ+ students and local artists. Events are curated by Lark Alder, the 2018-2020 Faculty Fellow at the SJSU PRIDE Center.

QuArtz also hosts weekly meetups at the PRIDE Center for students to create and share their art amongst peers. The group is currently collaborating on a creative toolkit for engaging in difficult questions and connecting across difference to build resilient communities.

All events are wheelchair accessible, individuals requiring other accommodations should contact the SJSU PRIDE Center at (408) 924-6157 or sjsupride@gmail.com.
